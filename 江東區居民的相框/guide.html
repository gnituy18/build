Build 1 — Assembly & Test Guide

XIAO ESP32-C6 + Waveshare 3.6″ e-Paper HAT+ (E) Spectra 6 panel + 3×AA battery + next-photo button. Each stage ends with a test — never move on, and never solder, until the current stage passes.

Rule 1 — test before solder. Stages 0–4 run entirely solderless on the breadboard with push-on connections. Stage 5 makes things permanent, re-testing after each joint group. This is what guarantees you never desolder.
Rule 2 — use only the white 9-pin labeled connector on the HAT, never the 40-pin holes. On this unit the 40-pin path is electrically dead: the controller answers but the panel never powers. The 9-pin header has its name printed beside every pin, so there is no counting and no pin-1 guessing.
Rule 3 — max 2–3 seconds of iron contact per joint. The first C6 was killed by holding the iron on it too long. If solder doesn't take: pull away, wait 30 seconds, add flux, retry. Tin every wire end first so joints take 2 seconds.

Parts

  • XIAO ESP32-C6 + 2.54 mm header pins
  • Waveshare 3.6″ e-Paper HAT+ (E) with its white 9-pin cable + FFC ribbon
  • Perfboard, breadboard, jumper wires
  • 4-pin tactile button
  • 1N5817 Schottky diode
  • 3×AA holder with alkaline batteries
  • USB-C cable, solder, flux

Stage 0 — Firmware and bare chip (USB only)

Solder header pins onto the XIAO (pins only — quick joints), push it into the breadboard, plug in USB:

idf.py erase-flash flash monitor
Pass: the log boots into provisioning and prints the SoftAP name build-1-XXXX. Chip, toolchain, and flash all proven before a single project wire exists.

Stage 1 — Panel, via the 9-pin breakout (solderless)

Connect the FFC ribbon from the panel into the HAT's connector (flip the latch, insert, close the latch). Plug the white 9-pin cable's free ends onto the XIAO header pins, matching the printed labels:

Power up over USB. Credentials were erased in Stage 0, so the firmware draws the setup screen — that exercises the entire panel path.

Pass: power-on completes in ~3 s, then a 15–25 s refresh with visible flashing, ending with “WiFi Setup / Join WiFi: build-1-XXXX / Then open: 192.168.4.1” readable on the panel.
Fail: refresh “done” in ~3 s with a blank screen = a data wire isn't landing (reseat DIN/CLK/CS/DC). Power-on hung or 35 s+ = power path problem (reseat VCC/GND/PWR, check the FFC latch — and make sure you're not on the 40-pin holes).

Stage 2 — WiFi setup and first photo (no new wiring)

On your phone: join the build-1-XXXX network shown on the panel, open the setup page, enter your home WiFi credentials.

Pass: log shows connect → NTP → HTTPS fetch of 120000 bytes → WiFi stopped; powering panel → real refresh → your photo on the panel → deep sleep.
Fail: wrong credentials → the panel itself shows the red “WiFi failed” screen and reboots into setup; just redo it.

Normal, not errors: after deep sleep the monitor prints Error: read failed: [Errno 6] — USB serial powers down with the chip.

Stage 3 — Button (solderless)

Push the tactile button into the breadboard across the center gap. Use two diagonal legs — same-side legs are internally shorted, which reads as always-pressed and boot-loops the device out of sleep, draining batteries. Jumper: one diagonal leg → D0, the other → GND.

With the device asleep after Stage 2, press the button once and judge by the panel, not the log: the fetch carries ?offset=N, so a button wake shows the next photo.

Pass: the panel refreshes and ends on a different photo than before, then sleeps again. (Second check: your server's access log shows /build/1/photo?offset=1.)
Fail: wakes by itself or loops = same-side legs, move one wire to the diagonal. Nothing happens at all = button not reaching D0/GND.
Why not the log? woke from button — offset now 1 prints in the first half-second after wake, before macOS re-detects the USB port — those lines are always lost. And re-running idf.py monitor resets the chip: that's a normal boot, not a button wake, so offset correctly stays put.

Stage 4 — Battery (solderless)

Battery red (+) → 1N5817 plain end; the grey-band end → VBUS (band always points at the XIAO). Battery black (−) → GND. Clips or breadboard rows for now.

Two invariants: never feed the battery into the 3V3 pin (bypasses the regulator), and never skip the diode — it blocks USB from charging the alkalines, which also means USB and battery may be connected at the same time. We use VBUS instead of the BAT pads because BAT runs through the lipo charger, which must never see alkalines.

Unplug USB. Press the button.

Pass: a full 15–25 s flashing refresh to the next photo on battery alone.
Fail: stall or half-drawn image = diode backwards, weak batteries, or loose GND.

Stage 5 — Solder it permanent

Everything works; you are only replacing push-on connections with solder, one group at a time, retesting after each.

  1. Mount the XIAO on the perfboard by its header pins — raised, so USB stays reachable and the module takes less heat.
  2. Make a GND junction: three things need GND (panel, button, battery) but the XIAO has one GND pin. One short wire from the XIAO GND pin to a free perfboard row; all three GND wires land on that row.
  3. Panel: solder the 9 cable wires (3V3, junction-GND, and the seven D-pins per the table). → rerun Stage 1's test.
  4. Button: solder its two diagonal-leg wires to D0 and the junction. → rerun Stage 3's test.
  5. Battery: solder red → diode → VBUS (band at the XIAO) and black → junction. → rerun Stage 4's test.
If a retest fails after soldering: the joint you just made is the suspect — look for a dull, grainy, blobby joint and reflow only that one (2 s of iron, a little fresh solder).

Final check

Leave it overnight on battery. At JST midnight it wakes itself and refreshes to the day's photo. Then case it — done.

Gifting

  1. Erase your WiFi before wrapping: idf.py erase-flash flash. The frame now has no credentials, so her very first boot goes straight to the setup screen — no red fail screen, no leftover network of yours.
  2. Take the batteries out and pack them separately. Inserting them is her "power on".
  3. Include a note, something like:
    裝上電池，等一分鐘，螢幕會出現設定說明，照著做就好。
    按鈕按一次就好，每個畫面要等一兩分鐘。
    之後每天半夜會自動換照片；按一下按鈕馬上換下一張。
Self-healing behavior to know: the setup screen stays up for 10 minutes, then the frame deep-sleeps to protect the battery (the panel keeps showing the instructions). A button press brings setup back for another 10 minutes. After she's configured it once, a temporary router outage costs at most one skipped day — the frame retries her saved WiFi on the next wake; it only waits in setup mode again if the connection keeps failing, and even then sleeps after 10 minutes.
